Privacy policy · Effective 2026-04-27

We collect nothing.

A short, honest privacy policy for an app that doesn't have any data to be careless with.

Plain English

Atrophy runs entirely on your iPhone. It has no servers, no analytics, no third-party SDKs, no tracking. The only network calls it makes are to Apple's StoreKit when you subscribe, restore a purchase, or check your subscription status. That's it. We do not give you a chatbot, and we do not give anyone your data — for the same reason: that is the entire point.

What stays on your device

Atrophy stores two things in your iPhone's local app storage (Apple's UserDefaults system):

Treatment enrollment date
The date you first opened the recovery program, used to compute "Day N of the program." Stored under the key atr.enrolledAt. Never leaves your device.
Lifetime-waitlist email (only if you provide one)
If you tap "Join the lifetime waitlist" and enter an email, that email is stored under atr.waitlistEmail. In v1.0 it is stored locally only — there is no server to send it to. Once the lifetime tier is priced and we have a way to email you, we will ask for your email again. You can clear this at any time by reinstalling the app or signing out from Settings.

That's everything. Your assessment results, your written responses, your scores, your prediction-vs-measurement gap — none of these leave your device, and they reset every cold launch by design.

What leaves your device

The only outbound network calls Atrophy makes are to Apple's StoreKit:

  • Fetching the price and availability of the monthly and annual subscriptions.
  • Initiating a purchase when you tap "Begin treatment."
  • Verifying your active subscription on app launch ("Restore purchases").

These calls are governed by Apple's privacy policy. Atrophy never sees your Apple ID, your payment method, or any personal billing information — Apple handles all of it and tells us only whether you have an active subscription.

What we don't do

  • No analytics. No PostHog, Mixpanel, Amplitude, Google Analytics, or anything similar.
  • No crash reporting beyond Apple's optional, opt-in TestFlight diagnostics.
  • No third-party SDKs. The only external library in v1 is Yams (a YAML parser, used to load the diagnostic content from a bundled file). It does not make network calls.
  • No advertising identifiers. No App Tracking Transparency prompt, because there's nothing to track.
  • No social sign-in. No accounts. No login.
  • No fingerprinting, no device identifiers, no IP-based geolocation.

Children

Atrophy is intended for adult knowledge workers. It is not directed at children under 13, does not knowingly collect anything from anyone, and is age-gated 12+ in the App Store age rating because it discusses mental-health-adjacent topics in a clinical-deadpan tone.

Changes to this policy

If we ever start collecting data — for example, if a future version syncs your assessment history to iCloud, or sends anonymized aggregate statistics to a server — this page will be updated and the change will be flagged in the app's release notes before the new behavior ships. We will not retroactively start collecting data from existing users without their explicit consent.
