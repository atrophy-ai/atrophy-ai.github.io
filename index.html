What it is

A self-administered iPhone diagnostic for knowledge workers who suspect they have begun to think in the cadence of a model. Five minutes. No account. No data leaves your device. The app delivers a 0–100 index, a tier diagnosis, and — most importantly — a Calibration Miss: the gap between how cooked you predicted you'd be and how cooked the writing actually shows you are.

If the index is elevated and you want to do something about it, treatment is a 7- to 60-day program of paper-only writing exercises. No AI tutor. No streaks. No leaderboard. No "you're crushing it." Just one prescription per day until you remember what your unaided draft used to feel like.

What it is not

  • Not a chatbot. There is no conversational AI inside the app. We do not give you a chatbot. That is the entire point.
  • Not a clinical diagnosis. The brand is medical-deadpan; the math is not. The atrophy index has no validated clinical referent. We say so on every result screen.
  • Not a tracker. No analytics SDK. No remote server. No third-party requests. See the privacy policy — it is short and accurate.
  • Not free forever. The diagnostic is yours to keep. Treatment is $5/month or $45/year. Terms.

The construct, briefly

The thing the app measures is not "AI use." Many calibrated heavy users of large language models retain full unaided performance and remain aware of when their output is theirs and when it isn't. The construct is the decoupling of confidence from output quality — the metacognitive miscalibration that the cognitive-science literature treats as the engine of skill atrophy. Polished output reads to the writer as evidence of competence, even when verification has quietly stopped happening. The longer this goes on, the harder it gets to write a bad first draft on purpose.
